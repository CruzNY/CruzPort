About Me

Who I am and what my interests are.

My name is Alexis Cruz and I am currently a senior at the New York City College of Technology majoring in Computer Systems. I'm studying to beocme a software engineer, but I also enjoy developing websites and tinkering with things that I don't really understand. My first programming langauge was Java, which I learned throughout college and from there I went on to learn Python and the Django framework along with other languages such as HTML/CSS, SQL, XML, BASH, and LaTex. One of my biggest interests is web accessibility. Developing properly structured and semantic websites so that people with disabilties can easily access them with their assistive technology. I believe that adding accessibility to your websites adds a degree of personality, it shows that you value and understand peoples needs and want to accommodate them. I've had the pleasure of interning over the summer at Usablenet. Usablenet is a technology and service company for digital accessibility; while there I worked closely with people of the disability community testing and going through use cases on various website for clients such as Toyota, Fresh Direct, Golden Corral and JC Penny. I learned that no user and assistive technology are the same and that there's a lot that goes into making a website accessible.

About this website

This is my frist project using Bulma, a Responsive CSS framework and Django a Python web developement framework. Both framework's concepts are easy to follow and really make it easy for the developer to get projects started and running. This website is still in development and I would like to add accesibility and javascript animations.
